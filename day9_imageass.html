Water

Hydration

Water is a colorless, odorless, and tasteless inorganic substance essential for all known forms of life, covering roughly 71% of the Earth's surface. Composed of two hydrogen atoms and one oxygen atom (\(\text{H}_{2}\text{O}\)), it acts as a universal solvent and constitutes a major portion of living organisms, including 70% of the human body. Beyond sustaining life through hydration, it is crucial for agriculture, industry, and climate regulation. While abundant on the planet, only a small, finite percentage is freshwater suitable for consumption, making its conservation vital to combat scarcity.
